# How to record semaphore data (easy guide)

Thanks for helping. This takes about 10 minutes the first time, then a couple of
minutes whenever you record. You do not need to know anything technical.

## Step 1: Install Python (one time only)

Go to this page and download the installer for your computer:

https://www.python.org/downloads/release/python-31011/

- On a **Mac**: scroll to the bottom, click "macOS 64-bit universal2 installer",
  open the downloaded file, and click Continue / Agree / Install.
- On **Windows**: click "Windows installer (64-bit)". When it opens, **check the
  box that says "Add Python to PATH"**, then click Install Now.

## Step 2: Unzip the folder I sent you

Double-click the `.zip` file I sent. It becomes a folder called
`semaphore-collector`. Open that folder.

## Step 3: Start it

- On a **Mac**: double-click **`start_mac.command`**.
  - If you see a warning that it cannot be opened, right-click the file, choose
    **Open**, then click **Open** again. (You only have to do this once.)
- On **Windows**: double-click **`start_windows.bat`**.

The first time, a black window appears and sets things up for a few minutes
(it downloads a small voice helper). Please leave it running.

Your computer will ask permission to use the **camera** and the **microphone** -
click Allow / OK for both.

## Step 4: Record

A camera window opens. In the corner is a chart of semaphore positions. At the
top it shows a word or letter to sign, like `hello` or `r`.

For each one:

1. Get into position.
2. **Say "next"** out loud. A short countdown plays ("1 Mississippi, 2
   Mississippi"), then it records.
3. Make the sign, and hold the final position for a second.
4. **Say "next"** again. It saves what you did and moves to the next word - all
   hands-free. Just keep going: sign, "next", sign, "next".

Keep your hands inside the camera view the whole time.

Do a few takes of each word, signing a little differently each time (a bit
faster, a bit slower). It automatically gives you whichever word it has the
fewest recordings of.

Prefer buttons? You can also press the keys shown at the bottom of the window
instead of talking.

## Step 5: Send the data back

When you are done, click the green **Export ZIP** button in the corner of the
window (or press the `z` key). This creates a file named like
`semaphore_data_2026-06-08.zip` inside the `semaphore-collector` folder.

Email that one file back to me. That is everything.

Thank you.
